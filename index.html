KHANKHAYAN

I began my career in architecture after completing a five-year Bachelor’s degree at King Mongkut’s Institute of Technology Ladkrabang (KMITL). My academic and professional journey has always centered around design, structure, and thoughtful problem-solving. Working on construction documentation at Crone Architects for 2.5 years and later at Cox Architects for 9 months strengthened my attention to detail, systems thinking, and ability to coordinate complex information across teams.

Through this experience, I discovered that what I enjoy most is not only designing spaces, but designing experiences, how people move, interact, and feel within a system. My passion for visuals, grids, patterns, color, and fine details naturally led me toward UX/UI design, where I can apply architectural thinking to digital environments

Architecture taught me to think in structures, flows, and human behavior. UX allows me to translate those same principles into interfaces that improve everyday interactions. I am particularly drawn to how thoughtful design can gently enhance daily routines and create meaningful, intuitive experiences for users.

I am now transitioning into UX/UI design to focus on creating human-centered digital products, bringing with me a strong foundation in design discipline, collaboration, and detail-oriented thinking developed through years of architectural practice.
